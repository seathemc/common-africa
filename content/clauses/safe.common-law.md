# Simple Agreement for Future Equity (SAFE) — Common-law

> **Status:** Draft v0.1 — for community review. Not legal advice. Adapt with qualified counsel before use.

## Background

The SAFE is an instrument developed by Y Combinator in 2013 (post-money form, 2018) to provide a fast, low-friction way for early-stage startups to receive capital in exchange for the right to future equity. African common-law jurisdictions (Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, South Africa) currently lack express statutory recognition; some (Nigeria SEC Rules 2013) classify it as debt, distorting accounting and tax treatment.

This drafting adapts the post-money SAFE for use under common-law African company regimes, with an explicit invocation of the labelled-startup framework where it exists.

## Parties

- **Company:** [Name], a company incorporated under the laws of [Jurisdiction] with registration number [Number] (the "Company").
- **Investor:** [Name / Entity].

## 1. Investment

In exchange for the payment by the Investor of [USD / NGN / KES / etc.] [Amount] (the "Purchase Amount"), the Company issues to the Investor the right to certain shares of the Company, subject to the terms of this agreement.

## 2. Definitions

- **"Equity Financing"** means a bona fide transaction or series of transactions with the principal purpose of raising capital, pursuant to which the Company issues and sells preferred shares at a fixed pre-money valuation.
- **"Liquidity Event"** means a Change of Control or an Initial Public Offering.
- **"Dissolution Event"** means a voluntary termination of operations, a general assignment for the benefit of the Company's creditors or any other liquidation, dissolution or winding-up of the Company.
- **"Post-Money Valuation Cap"** means [USD / NGN] [Amount].
- **"Discount Rate"** means [80%] (i.e. a 20% discount).
- **"SAFE Price"** means the price per share equal to the Post-Money Valuation Cap divided by the Company's Capitalisation immediately prior to the Equity Financing.
- **"Discount Price"** means the price per share of the Standard Preferred Shares sold in the Equity Financing multiplied by the Discount Rate.

## 3. Conversion events

### 3.1 Equity Financing

If there is an Equity Financing before the termination of this SAFE, on the closing of that Equity Financing, this SAFE will automatically convert into the number of shares of SAFE Preferred Shares equal to the Purchase Amount divided by the Conversion Price (being the lower of the SAFE Price and the Discount Price).

### 3.2 Liquidity Event

If there is a Liquidity Event before the termination of this SAFE, the Investor will, at their option, either (i) receive a cash payment equal to the Purchase Amount, or (ii) automatically receive a number of ordinary shares equal to the Purchase Amount divided by the Liquidity Price.

### 3.3 Dissolution Event

If there is a Dissolution Event before the termination of this SAFE, the Investor will receive a cash distribution equal to the Purchase Amount, payable in priority to ordinary shareholders.

## 4. Representations

### 4.1 Company

The Company is duly incorporated, has full power to enter into this SAFE, and has obtained all necessary consents (including, where applicable, recognition under the [Nigeria Startup Act / Kenya Startup Act / Ghana Startup Act] as a labelled startup permitted to issue convertible instruments).

### 4.2 Investor

The Investor has full legal capacity to enter into this SAFE and is acquiring the SAFE for their own account.

## 5. Statutory recognition

The parties acknowledge that this instrument is intended to operate as a convertible security under the prevailing securities laws of [Jurisdiction]. Where the Company is a labelled startup under [the applicable Startup Act], this SAFE shall be treated as an equity-linked instrument and not as debt for accounting, tax, or regulatory purposes, in accordance with [the applicable Startup Act provision].

## 6. Miscellaneous

This SAFE is governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction]. Any dispute shall be resolved by [chosen forum]. The Company shall maintain the Investor's name in its register of holders of convertible securities.

## Implementation notes

- **Nigeria:** Until the SEC issues guidance, the SAFE is best paired with a one-page side letter clarifying its non-debt character. The Common framework recommends an SEC rule amendment recognising SAFEs issued by NSA-labelled startups.
- **Kenya:** Capital Markets Authority has not classified SAFEs; cautious counsel often re-cast the instrument as a convertible loan with zero-coupon. The model above can be used directly once the Startup Bill 2022 is enacted with explicit hybrid-instrument recognition.
- **Ghana:** Securities Industry Act constraints currently push founders to issue SAFEs via offshore entities; Ghana's strong fintech-passport posture makes it a natural early adopter of express SAFE recognition.
- **South Africa:** SAFEs are commonly used; the open question is exchange control treatment of the Investor's claim where the Investor is non-resident.
